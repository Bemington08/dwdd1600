Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and later the 3rd President of the U.S.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress whose large, central signature made his name a synonym for "signature."
Benjamin Franklin
The oldest signer at age 70 and a world-renowned scientist and diplomat.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence on the drafting committee and the 2nd President of the U.S.
Samuel Adams
A leading revolutionary known as the "Firebrand of the Revolution" for his activism in Massachusetts.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four great state papers of the U.S., including the Articles of Confederation and the Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The wealthiest signer and the last surviving member of the group, living until 1832.
Button Gwinnett
A Georgia delegate whose signature is among the rarest and most valuable due to his early death in a duel.
Edward Rutledge
The youngest signer of the Declaration of Independence at age 26.
